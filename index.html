My Background

In 2017, I graduated from Sonoma State University with B.A.s in Economics and Pure Mathematics, and a B.S. in Statistics. I started at SSU in 2012, the same year I graduated from Maria Carrillo High chool and completed the rank of Eagle in the Boy Scouts of America. While I enrolled as an Economics major, as my fascination with Econometrics grew, I added more degrees. What free time I had was devoted to the Economics and Mathematics clubs, Greek Life, tutoring and working as a manager at a local movie theater.

A few months after graduating from SSU, I moved to New York City to start the Master's program in Economics at New York University. While at NYU, I was introduced to Bayesian Econometrics and Machine Learning. The modeling techniques from these areas became regular staples of many of my class projects. In the months following my graduation from NYU in the spring of 2019, I began studying and researching full-time in prepaprtion for starting in a PhD program.

It is my goal to become a professor and researcher of Bayesian Machine Learning and Econometrics.
