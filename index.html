Welcome to my Research Portfolio!

My name is Riley Conlon. I am a recent NYU grad and amateur Econometrics researcher. I hold an MA in Economics from NYU, a BA in Economics, a BS in Statistics and a BA in Pure Mathematics from Sonoma State University. My research interests are in Bayesian Regression Analysis, Machine Learning, and Computer Science applications to Economics. I set up this website as a way to show off some of the research projects I have completed. I hope that you find this resporistory to be helpful.

My Background

In 2017, I graduated from Sonoma State University with B.A.s in Economics and Pure Mathematics, and a B.S. in Statistics. I started at SSU in 2012, the same year I graduated from Maria Carrillo High chool and completed the rank of Eagle in the Boy Scouts of America. While I applied to SSU as an Economics major, as my fascination with Econometrics grew, I added more degrees. What free time I had was devoted to the Economics and Mathematics clubs, Greek Life, tutoring and working as a manager at a local movie theater.

After graduating from SSU, I moved to New York City to start the Master's program in Economics at New York University. While at NYU, I was introduced to Bayesian Econometrics and Machine Learning. The modeling techniques from these areas became regular staples of many of my class projects. Even though I was enrolled in the Economics program, I took as many classes in Math and Statistics from NYU's Courant School.

In 2019, I graduated with an MA in Economics from NYU. From there, I began temporary work as a research assistant, while working on my own research projects. It was during this time that I also began studying topics in Computer Science, Mathematical Physics and Finance.
